# Discovery Question Bank

Use these questions to probe the most important gaps in the operator's idea. Select 4–6 per session — do not ask all of them.

---

## User Questions

- Who is the primary user of this product? Describe them specifically (job title, context, behavior).
- Is this targeting consumers (B2C), businesses (B2B), or both?
- How does this person currently solve the problem you're addressing? What tools do they use today?
- What would make this person switch to your product?
- How tech-savvy is your typical user?

---

## Problem Questions

- What specific moment of frustration does your user experience? Describe the scenario.
- How often does this problem occur? Daily, weekly, monthly?
- What are the consequences when this problem isn't solved?
- Is this problem primarily about saving time, saving money, reducing stress, or enabling something new?
- Have you spoken to any potential users? What did they say?

---

## Market & Timing Questions

- Why is this the right time to build this?
- Are you aware of any existing solutions? Why are they falling short?
- Is there a regulatory or technology change driving this opportunity?

---

## Constraint Questions

- What's your target platform — mobile app, web app, desktop, or all?
- Do you have a target launch timeline?
- Is there a technical stack preference or constraint?
- Are there budget constraints that affect scope?
- Will you be building this yourself or hiring a team?

---

## Scope Questions

- What is the single most important thing your product must do in V1?
- What features are you explicitly not building in V1?
- What does success look like at 3 months post-launch?
- What does a failed launch look like to you?

---

## Distribution Questions

- How will users find this product? (Search, referral, sales, etc.)
- Is there a natural distribution channel tied to the problem context?
- Do you need to integrate with any existing platforms or tools?
