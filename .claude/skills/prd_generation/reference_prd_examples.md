# PRD Writing Examples — Strong vs. Weak

## Section 2: Problem Statement

**Weak:**
> "Small business owners struggle with loyalty programs and want better tools."

Problems: vague, no specifics, no emotional grounding.

**Strong:**
> "Independent coffee shop owners lose 20–30% of first-time customers who never return, often because there's no mechanism to reward repeat visits. Paper punch cards are easily forgotten, lost, or gamed. Existing digital solutions like Square Loyalty require a Square POS subscription, locking out the majority of indie operators who use different systems. This product exists because there is no lightweight, POS-agnostic loyalty tool built specifically for the needs of a solo-operated coffee shop."

## Section 4: User Personas

**Weak:**
> "Maya is a small business owner who wants to grow her customer base."

Problems: no role, no frustration, no specificity.

**Strong:**
> **Persona: Maya Chen**
> - **Role:** Owner-operator of a single-location independent coffee shop
> - **Context:** 38, runs the shop herself with 2 part-time staff, opens at 6am daily
> - **Goal:** Build a base of loyal regulars who come back 3–4x per week and feel recognized
> - **Frustration:** Has tried paper punch cards but customers always forget them; has heard of Square Loyalty but doesn't use Square and doesn't want to pay $45/month for just the loyalty feature
> - **Quote:** "I know my regulars by name, but I can't reward them for it in any scalable way."

## Section 11: Success Metrics

**Weak:**
> "We want to grow our user base and make customers happy."

Problems: not measurable, no targets, no timeframes.

**Strong:**
> **North Star Metric:**
> % of enrolled customers who scan their stamp card at least once per week — target: 40% within 60 days of a shop going live
>
> | Metric | Definition | Target | Timeframe |
> |---|---|---|---|
> | Shop activation rate | % of signed-up shops that issue their first stamp within 7 days | >70% | Month 1 |
> | Monthly stamp scan rate | Average scans per active shop per month | >80 | Month 2 |
> | Customer retention lift | % increase in repeat visits vs. pre-launch baseline | >15% | Month 3 |
> | Churn rate | % of paying shops that cancel per month | <5% | Month 3+ |

---

## Section 12: Risks and Assumptions

**Weak:**
> "There is a risk that users don't like the product."

Problems: too vague to be actionable, no mitigation.

**Strong:**
> **Risk: Adoption friction at point of sale**
> - Likelihood: H | Impact: H
> - The product requires customers to scan a QR code at the register. If this adds even 10 seconds to checkout, busy shop owners will abandon it.
> - Mitigation: Design check-in flow to complete in under 3 seconds. Run usability test during checkout rush before launch.

> **Assumption: Indie shop owners will pay $19–29/month for this**
> - This is an untested pricing assumption. If shops are unwilling to pay, the revenue model fails.
> - Validation: run pre-launch price sensitivity survey with 20 prospective users.
